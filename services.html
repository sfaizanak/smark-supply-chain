International Supply Chain

An international supply chain involves the global network of suppliers, manufacturers, logistics providers, and customers. It requires managing and coordinating activities across different countries, cultures, and time zones. International supply chains are complex and involve various stakeholders, including:

  • Sourcing raw materials or goods from suppliers around the world.
  • Managing the movement of goods across borders, including transportation, warehousing, and customs clearance.
  • Producing goods in different countries to take advantage of lower labor costs, skilled workforce, or proximity to markets.
  • Managing the export and import of goods, including compliance with regulations, tariffs, and trade agreements.

National Supply Chain

A national supply chain, on the other hand, operates within the boundaries of a single country. It involves managing and coordinating activities with local suppliers, manufacturers, logistics providers, and customers. National supply chains are critical for meeting domestic demand and supporting economic growth

  • It plays a crucial role in supporting the national economy by facilitating domestic production, trade, and consumption.
  • Managing the movement of goods across borders, including transportation, warehousing, and customs clearance.
  • It involves collaboration among local suppliers, manufacturers, logistics providers, and distributors to meet domestic demand efficiently.
  • National supply chains are often designed to respond quickly to local market changes, policy adjustments, and regional disruptions, ensuring stability in supply and demand..
